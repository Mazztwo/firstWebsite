The moon has eight phases that we have named based on what it looks like here on Earth. The six phases are determined on the moon's position relative to the Sun and Earth.

    The phases are as follows:
  1. New Moon: no light visible on the moon
  2. Waxing Crescent: light on right, crescent shape
  3. First Quarter: right half of moon illuminated
  4. Waxing Gibbous: more than half of moon is illuminated, shadow on left
  5. Full Moon: full face of moon is illuminated
  6. Waning Gibbous: light on left, more than half of moon is illuminated
  7. Third Quarter: left half of moon illuminated
  8. Waning Crescent: light on left, crescent shape
